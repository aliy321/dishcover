# Dishcover — Monetisation Strategy

## Core Philosophy

Dish-level data is Dishcover's moat. Google Maps knows *where* a stall is.
Dishcover knows *what's worth ordering there*. That's what businesses and tourists will pay for.

---

## Revenue Streams

### 1. Business Claim (Primary)

Stall owners pay to verify and manage their listing.

**Flow:**
```
Stall owner sees their listing on Dishcover
  ↓
"Is this your stall? Claim it" CTA on listing page
  ↓
Verify ownership (phone/email)
  ↓
Access merchant dashboard:
  - Update menu, hours, photos
  - Reply to reviews
  - View listing analytics (views, saves, clicks)
```

**Pricing tiers:**
```
Free (always)
  - Basic listing auto-generated by Dishcover
  - Cannot edit or respond to reviews
  - Shows "Claim this stall" prompt

Starter — $9–$15/month  (or $99/year)
  - Claim + edit listing
  - Upload photos
  - Reply to reviews

Pro — $29–$39/month
  - Everything in Starter
  - Analytics dashboard
  - Featured placement in category section
  - Priority placement in Roulette results
```

**Sales pitch to hawker owners:**
> "For less than the cost of one plate of food per day, you appear at the top
> when someone nearby searches for your dish."

**Why they pay:** more accurate listing = more customers walking in. Direct ROI.

**Pricing note:** $49/month is too steep for hawker economics.
Typical hawker profit: $600–$2,400/month. Annual $99 framing is much easier to sell.

---

### 2. Featured Placement

Stalls pay to appear prominently in discovery surfaces.

**Placement options:**
- Top slot in category section (Hawker, Cafe, etc.) on home screen
- Sponsored suggestion in Roulette results
- Top result in Search
- "Dishcover Pick" badge on listing

**Pricing:** $50–$200/month depending on placement and city

---

### 3. City Guide / Tourism Partnerships

Curated collections sold to tourism boards, hotels, airlines.

**Examples:**
- Singapore Tourism Board: "Top 50 Hawker Dishes in SG" in-app collection
- Hotel concierge: "Nearby food guide" embedded for hotel guests
- Airlines: in-flight app food discovery for destination cities
- Klook / Airbnb Experiences: food tour integrations

**Deal size:** $5,000–$20,000 per partnership (one-time or annual)

**Why this works:** Dishcover has dish-level curation that generic map apps don't.

---

### 4. Dish-Level Data & Insights (Long-term)

Sell anonymised trend data to F&B brands and researchers.

**Buyers:**
- F&B ingredient brands (what dishes using their product are trending)
- Market researchers (what tourists vs locals eat, by district)
- Property developers (which hawker centres are gaining/losing popularity)

**Format:** Monthly subscription reports or one-off research exports

**Pricing:** $500–$5,000/month

## Revenue Projection (Singapore, Year 1 Target)

```
500  claimed stalls  × $99/yr    =  $49,500/yr   (~$4,125/mo)
50   featured stalls × $100/mo   =   $5,000/mo
2    tourism deals   × $10,000   =  $20,000/yr   (~$1,667/mo)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Estimated MRR (Year 1 target)    ~  $10,800/mo
                                 =  ~$130K ARR
```

*Note: conservative estimate. Scales significantly with city expansion.*

## The Growth Flywheel

```
More users → more reviews + data
    ↓
More accurate dish-level insights
    ↓
More valuable to stall owners + brands
    ↓
More revenue → hire city editors → expand to KL, Bangkok, etc.
    ↓
More users
```

---

## Expansion Model

| Phase | Market | Curation Model |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| 1 | Singapore | Dishcover team curates everything |
| 2 | Southeast Asia (KL, Jakarta, Bangkok) | Local city editors / partners per city |
| 3 | Global | Hybrid: platform seeds via Google Places API, community fills gaps |

**Key insight:** The app architecture doesn't change across phases.
Only the data ingestion pipeline changes. The data model works globally.
